Majestic peaks, like the fangs of an ancient deity, pierce the leaden sky. The air hums with whispers carried by icy winds from the mountain passes. In black crevices where no light penetrates, creatures older than the rock itself rustle with their scales.

Foul vapors rise from the swamp, where centuries-old rot devours even the memory of light. Crooked trees, like the twisted fingers of drowned men, cling to the poisonous mist, whispering curses in the language of mold. From the black, bubbling sludge, empty eye sockets slowly rise to the surface, watching the living.

Frozen in eternal darkness, the ruins lie beneath the weight of the silent ocean. Giant shadows born in the abyss float through the broken arches. The whispers of a lost race, captured in carved statues, tell of gods sleeping in stone sarcophagi.
